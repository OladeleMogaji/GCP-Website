Surveying & Engineering

Accurate measurement in the field is the foundation every design, boundary, and construction decision rests on.

Services

Core capabilities

Standard land surveying and engineering-support services this practice is built around. [Mark which of these you're currently able to offer vs. still training toward, or remove any that don't apply.]

Boundary

Boundary Surveys

Establishing and marking legal property lines by researching deeds, locating existing monuments, and taking precise field measurements to resolve boundary questions.

Topography

Topographic Surveys

Mapping the natural and built features of a site — elevation, contours, structures, utilities, and vegetation — to support design and planning decisions.

Construction

Construction Staking

Translating design drawings into physical markers on-site, so construction crews build to the correct location, elevation, and alignment.

Title & Development

ALTA/NSPS Land Title Surveys & Subdivision Platting

Detailed surveys that meet lending and title-insurance standards, plus platting support for dividing land into lots for development.

Elevation

Elevation Certificates

Documenting a structure's elevation relative to flood zones for insurance and regulatory compliance.

Planning support

Site Development Support

Providing the survey groundwork — existing conditions, grading data, easements — that engineers and planners need before design begins.

Process

How a survey project moves forward

A consistent sequence, borrowed from the field itself — each stage is a benchmark the next stage is measured against.

Research & Reconnaissance

Reviewing deeds, prior surveys, and public records before ever stepping on site.

Field Data Collection

On-site measurement using GNSS/GPS and total station equipment to capture precise coordinates.

Processing & Drafting

Reducing raw field data into a drawn, checked, and annotated survey.

Deliverable & Review

Final plat or report handed off, with findings walked through in plain language.
